ABOUT

The event will start at the bottom of Mount San Antonio LA. Bike up/down the mountain. There will be the option to hire mountain bikes on arrival. There will be three levels, Beginner, Beginners will only go up half the mountain with long breaks to regain energy, Intermediate, Intermediate bikers will go three quarters up the mountain and can go at their own pace. Finally Expert, these riders can go up the mountain at their own speed and do not need to follow any instruction and they can choose their own path.

You can take as long as you need to get to the top of the mountain where you will camp in tents provided at the top of the mountain or you can bring your own. The next day you will make your way back down the mountain. The event is aimed at outdoor enthusiasts and experienced mountain bikers in the sport. There will also be lower levels to try and teach people how it all works and hopefully turn it into a hobby for them to do. The motivation to continue with the hobby is that when you sign up to the event you are given the option to buy a mountain bike with a discount, bike services will also be available for a discounted price. Local competitions will take place in the future in LA to give people motivation to work towards something.

Beginner - 9am

THE EVENT IS COMPLETELY FREE

Beginner

The beginner trail will be the easiest of the 3. Beginners will be slow paced and can take plenty of breaks, beginners will be on very shallow slopes and the surface wont be too difficult.

Intermediate

The intermediate trail will require a bit of fitness and some previous experience mountain biking. The intermediate riders will go faster and take less breaks.

Expert

The expert riders will be expected to ride tough trails. Expert riders will take short but frequent breaks, ride fast up and down the hill. Riders will ride technical, rocky uneven surfaces.

Location

The event will take place in Los Angeles as they have a lot of mountains. The mountain that you will be climbing will be Mount San Antonio as it is the biggest mountain in LA standing at 3,069 Metres.

Meeting Point
